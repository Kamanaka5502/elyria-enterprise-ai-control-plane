# Pilot Corridor Plan

## Purpose

The pilot corridor turns an AI idea or prototype into a controlled, evidence-backed pilot path.

It prevents enterprise AI from jumping directly from experimentation into production consequence without governance, authority, telemetry, and revalidation.

---

## Corridor Stages

| Stage | Purpose | Exit Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Idea | Identify business objective and AI capability. | Business owner and use case defined. |
| Discovery | Classify risk, data, identity, model, RAG, and agent scope. | Initial control gaps documented. |
| Sandbox | Evaluate use case through ADMIT / HOLD / REFUSE / REVALIDATE model. | Sandbox decision recorded. |
| Pilot | Deploy in limited controlled scope. | Telemetry, controls, and owner review active. |
| Production Candidate | Confirm readiness evidence and operational ownership. | Readiness checklist completed. |
| Production | Advance only if governance remains valid. | Monitoring and revalidation active. |

---

## Pilot Corridor Output

A complete pilot corridor should produce:

- use-case classification
- data scope map
- owner map
- authority map
- control matrix
- RAG readiness notes where applicable
- agent/tool-use boundary notes where applicable
- sandbox decision
- deployment readiness checklist
- revalidation triggers
- executive decision summary

---

## Corridor Rule

```text
No production movement without ownership, authority, data scope, control evidence, observability, and revalidation triggers.
```

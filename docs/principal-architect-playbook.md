# Principal Architect Playbook

## Purpose

This playbook shows how a Principal Architect leads enterprise AI adoption from ambiguity to governed delivery.

The objective is not to slow teams down. The objective is to create a repeatable path where AI systems can move faster because architecture, authority, data scope, controls, evaluation, observability, and revalidation are clear.

---

## Engagement Sequence

```text
1. Executive discovery
2. AI use-case classification
3. Data and authority mapping
4. Platform and integration review
5. Risk and control design
6. Evaluation and readiness model
7. Architecture decision records
8. Delivery roadmap
9. Production movement gate
10. Telemetry and revalidation model
```

---

## Executive Discovery Questions

- What AI capability is the organization trying to enable?
- What business outcome does leadership expect?
- What data will the system rely on?
- Who is accountable for the system after deployment?
- What decision or operational movement could the system influence?
- Could the AI system trigger access, payment, customer communication, production change, compliance exposure, or safety impact?
- What current governance process exists?
- What does the organization need to prove before production movement?

---

## Principal Architect Deliverables

| Deliverable | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Target-state architecture | Connect business objective to secure and governable AI delivery. |
| Control matrix | Map risks to required controls and evidence. |
| Architecture Decision Records | Preserve rationale, trade-offs, assumptions, and ownership. |
| Delivery roadmap | Sequence implementation across teams and dependencies. |
| Production readiness model | Define what must exist before deployment. |
| Revalidation model | Define when prior approval no longer holds. |
| Executive narrative | Translate technical architecture into business confidence. |

---

## Reusable Assets

A Principal Architect should leave behind assets that improve the organization beyond one project:

- reference architectures
- workshop templates
- decision frameworks
- control matrices
- ADR templates
- AI readiness checklists
- agent/tool-use control models
- RAG readiness patterns
- observability patterns
- revalidation triggers

---

## Operating Standard

A successful AI architecture engagement leaves the client with a clear answer to:

```text
What can move, what cannot move, who has authority, what data is involved, what controls exist, what evidence proves readiness, and what changes force revalidation?
```

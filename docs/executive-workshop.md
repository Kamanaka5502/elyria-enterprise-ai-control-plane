# Executive Workshop

## 90-Minute Enterprise AI Governance Workshop

This workshop is designed for CIO, CISO, CTO, data, privacy, risk, product, and AI leaders.

The purpose is to move from fragmented AI ambition into a governed architecture path.

---

## Agenda

| Time | Topic |
|---|---|
| 0-10 min | Business objective and AI adoption context |
| 10-25 min | AI use-case inventory and impact classification |
| 25-40 min | Data scope, sensitivity, and ownership |
| 40-55 min | Identity, access, agent/tool use, and authority boundaries |
| 55-70 min | Evaluation, observability, and production readiness |
| 70-85 min | Decision framework: ADMIT / HOLD / REFUSE / REVALIDATE |
| 85-90 min | Next steps and architecture deliverables |

---

## Core Questions

- What AI initiatives are active or planned?
- Which systems are advisory, customer-facing, production-critical, or agentic?
- What data is used or retrieved?
- Who owns approval and operational accountability?
- What tools, actions, or workflows can AI trigger?
- What evidence proves readiness?
- What monitoring exists after deployment?
- What changes force revalidation?

---

## Workshop Outputs

- use-case classification map
- data scope inventory
- authority and ownership map
- agent/tool-use boundary notes
- production readiness gaps
- initial control matrix
- recommended architecture path
- next-step delivery backlog

---

## Executive Close

The workshop should end with a clear statement:

```text
This AI movement is ADMITTED, HELD, REFUSED, or requires REVALIDATION based on current authority, data scope, control coverage, evaluation evidence, and operational readiness.
```
